The Kingdom of Mercia.

Designing a new kingdom for a post-Brexit Britain in need of change.

What could a new kingdom look like? What would it sound like? How would its citizens interact with it? This project explores these questions by branding an imaginary kingdom born out of a post-Brexit breakdown of Britain. The Kingdom of Mercia is an explorative concept which seeks to answer the questions of how to design and brand a nation.

Mercia forms after Britain subdivides into its old kingdoms. It is developed out of a realisation that cultural exchange is a crucial ingredient for a successful country, so the values of diversity and global outreach are at its heart.

Inclusivity, openness and diversity are the three key aspects which define the brand – it is inclusive of a wide group of people from varying backgrounds, with the resources available for total government transparency.

The branding embraces and utilises a variety of immigrant languages, which is a key indicator of an openness to immigration and different cultures. Immigration cannot be portrayed as a ‘corruption' of the values of the state when it is at the heart of it.

The citizens of the kingdom are therefore exposed to different languages constantly and it becomes part of the visual language of the state. The visual language and outputs implies that every citizen is valuable and important.
