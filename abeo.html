Abeo!

A website for exploring West African food and culinary cultures.

INDEPENDENT UNIVERSITY PROJECT.

West African cooking can feel inaccessible to British home-cooks: ingredients can be hard to access, recipes and techniques can feel unfamiliar and there is limited in-depth information in English online.

This project was one strand of a wider project examining how West African food could be successfully introduced into mainstream British taste. View the two other sister projects here and here.

Abeo! seeks to fill this gap by creating an online platform to explore West African food culture, with content relevant both to complete newcomers to West African cooking and those who have been cooking and eating it their entire lives.

Users can dive into all aspects of culinary culture: ingredients, recipes and principles of cooking. All elements of the website focus on creating a culture of accessibility. Users, for example, can discover in-depth information about commonly used ingredients, track down local suppliers or learn about substitutions.

I’m forever frustrated by the state of so many recipe pages online: they’re often full of unnecessary and unwarranted information (“my husband normally hates peas, but loves them in this recipe!!”) and take huge amounts of scrolling just to get to the elements you want — the ingredients and the method. My pages are cleaner, more focused and easy to navigate.

Each of the recipe pages on Abeo! start with a short, informative introduction, with further thoughts and notes hidden under a ‘read more’. If an ingredient or technique could use more explanation, it is highlighted — the user can click on it to bring in annotations with further explanation. The recipes themselves are very visual, offering a helping hand to those who may find the ingredients unfamiliar.

To introduce a wider audience to the website, free newspapers filled with the most accessible content and articles from the website are printed and distributed to independent retailers, cafés and restaurants.
