I'm Ella Marke a graphic designer and copywriter who is passionate about design which helps to make the world a better place. I love exploring the intersections of design, society and history in order to create rich, concept-focused solutions which are conscious of their context and relationship with their audience.

I particuarly love working with projects which involve branding, web design, theatre design, food industry, editorial layout. But this certainly doesn’t mean I’m not interested in other projects—tell me about what you need and I’ll tell you what I can do about it.

I use a rich, research-led process. It’s important to me that all the work I produce doesn’t just look great, but comes from a smart concept led by loads of contextual research. If you want to see case studies of how I’ve applied research to my work in the past, take a look over here.

The way I work changes project to project, but it generally fits within a specifc structure which I’ve found leads to the most interesting and thoughtful outcomes.

One

Understanding the brief. Some projects will have detailed briefs from the get-go, with all the information I’ll need to get started. Other times, there may not be a written brief at all—just a problem that needs to be solved. Either way, at this stage I’ll work through the problem and get to the bottom of exactly what the goals of this project will be. This includes looking at timescales, deliverables and pricing.

Two

Beginning research. Whilst I keep up research throughout the length of the project, the bulk of it comes at this stage. Although this varies depending on the type of project and its scope, I tend to break down my research into different sections, some which may be more relevant than others in different projects:

Historical / socio-political / aesthetic / commercial.

Three

Concept creation. At this stage, I’ll begin to formalise different concepts and approaches that would work to solve your design problem. This normally means rough sketches and some explanation of my thinking. It gives us a chance to work together to create a soloution that’s going to look beautiful and work beautifully.

Four

Designing. Here, we’ll pick a route to go down and I’ll spend time making the idea we visualised a reality, sending you updates along the way. This is when things start looking like the final product.

Five

Adjustments and finalisation. I’ll present you with the designs I have so far and we can discuss any changes you might want made. When you’re happy with the product, I’ll give you the final results. Job well done!
